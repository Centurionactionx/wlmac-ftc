A young program on the rise.

WLMAC Robotics is the student-run FIRST Tech Challenge program at William Lyon Mackenzie CI. Two seasons in, we field two competitive teams, build robots from scratch, and run STEM outreach across the region. FTC turns a school year into a full engineering cycle: design reviews, fabrication, code sprints, competition strategy. Students graduate having shipped real hardware.

The robots, the outreach, the budget: everything on this page was planned and executed by students. The school hosts us; sponsors make the season possible.

We compete in FIRST Tech Challenge, the international robotics league where teams of students design and build 18-inch robots to play a new game each year. Matches are won in alliances, seasons are won in the shop.

27964

Outreach

200+ students reached through six events and three partner schools: our Science and Engineering Olympics, the Library Open House, Grade 9 Night, and community workshops across North York. We're building the local STEM pipeline season by season.

Competition

FTC gives students engineering they can hold, run, and break. Across 6+ competitions in our second year, both teams tested months of build work, strategy, driving, and code against the region's best.
